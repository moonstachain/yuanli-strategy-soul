## 7. Scoring Rubric

Each coded record is scored 0-3 on five dimensions.

| Score | Meaning |
|---:|---|
| 0 | Not present |
| 1 | Weak hint |
| 2 | Clear signal |
| 3 | Strong field evidence |

Record-level scoring:

| Dimension | Question |
|---|---|
| Delivery clarity | Is the delivery workflow visible? |
| Commercial signal | Is there order, payment, budget, or follow-up seriousness? |
| Repeatability | Can this be repeated as SOP, template, agent workflow, or product? |
| Leverage | Does it reduce dependence on human time? |
| Data/asset compounding | Does it generate reusable data, case, template, or playbook? |

Record quality levels:

```text
0-4: weak record
5-8: usable record
9-12: strong record
13-15: B3 model evidence candidate
```

## 8. Chain-level Acceptance Criteria

Minimum pass:

```text
5+ coded B3 evidence records.
3+ delivery/workflow/productization signals.
2+ revenue/order/purchase/cashflow signals.
2+ repeatability/SOP/human-machine split signals.
1+ profit-container candidate.
```

Evidence strength decision:

```text
B3 Evidence can move from medium to medium+ only if minimum pass is met.
B3 cannot move to strong until controlled client/user validation or real repeat-use outcome is completed.
```
